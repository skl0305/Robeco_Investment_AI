Portfolio Construction and Institutional Considerations: Strategic India Exposure

Investing in CLINT offers institutional investors diversified, yet targeted, exposure to India's high-growth real estate sector, a crucial component for long-term alpha generation. The Trust's focus on IT, industrial, logistics, and data centers aligns with secular growth themes, providing a hedge against traditional real estate cyclicality. Its defensive capital structure, characterized by a healthy 42.3% gearing ratio and a high proportion of 77.2% fixed-rate debt, offers stability in volatile markets and mitigates interest rate risk. CLINT provides attractive yield (trailing dividend yield of 6.23%) coupled with capital appreciation potential, making it suitable for income-oriented and growth-at-a-reasonable-price mandates. Its correlation with broader global real estate indices is relatively low, offering valuable diversification benefits. Position sizing should reflect the high conviction in the thesis, allocating 1.5-2.0% of the core portfolio, with incremental additions on any short-term market dislocations that fail to account for the strong fundamental trajectory and impending catalysts.

Proprietary Monitoring Framework: Tracking Key Alpha-Driving Indicators

Our monitoring system for CLINT will focus on leading indicators that signal thesis validation or potential deterioration, rather than lagging financial statements alone. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to track include: 1) Quarterly rental reversions, particularly for new and renewed leases, to confirm pricing power and demand strength; 2) Leasing progress and pre-commitments for new data center and industrial assets to assess pipeline execution; 3) Updates on capital recycling initiatives, specifically the divestment of data centers to a sponsor-managed fund by Q4 2025, as a measure of proactive capital management; 4) Occupancy rates across the core portfolio to ensure continued tenant demand; and 5) Movement in the Indian Rupee (INR) vs. SGD to monitor FX impact on DPU (given 45.8% unhedged exposure). We will also monitor competitor activity in key micro-markets to assess any shifts in competitive dynamics or pricing power. Deviations from these benchmarks, either positive or negative, will trigger a re-evaluation of the investment thesis and potential adjustments to our position size.

Catalyst Timeline and Dynamic Exit Strategy

We foresee several key catalysts materializing over the next 12-18 months that will drive market recognition and share price appreciation. The most immediate catalyst is the Q4 2025 earnings release, expected to show initial revenue contributions from the first data center and potentially provide further guidance on the strategic capital recycling program. This will serve as a crucial validation point for our differentiated view on accretive growth. Subsequent quarters in 2026 will reveal continued DPU growth, reinforcing the underlying operational strength and countering the previous non-cash related net income dip. As the pipeline assets come online and are fully leased, we anticipate a gradual but sustained re-rating of CLINT's valuation multiples, aligning it more closely with higher-growth "new economy" REITs. Our exit strategy is dynamic: we will trim positions if the unit price reaches our S$1.31 target ahead of schedule without new accretive catalysts, or if our monitoring framework signals a significant deterioration in fundamental drivers (e.g., sustained negative rental reversions, unexpected delays in major projects). Conversely, should new, highly accretive acquisitions be announced or an acceleration in India's digital economy surpass our bull case expectations, we would consider increasing our position to capture further alpha, potentially revising our target to S$1.50+. This proactive approach ensures optimal risk-adjusted returns.
