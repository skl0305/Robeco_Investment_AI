The overall Total Assets for FLCT increased only marginally by +2.9% YoY to $7.1B in FY2024 from $6.9B in FY2023, signaling a deceleration in asset base growth. Competitive Landscape: Capital Access Differentiating Survivors from Value Traps

The current industry environment is intensifying the competitive landscape, fundamentally differentiating REITs with strong balance sheets and diversified capital sources from those reliant on traditional debt, like FLCT. As debt markets tighten and cost of debt rises (e.g., FLCT's 2.9% in 1QFY2025), access to cheap capital, a previous competitive advantage, is disappearing. This is forcing smaller, highly-leveraged players into dilutive equity raises or asset divestitures at unfavorable terms. The market is shifting towards larger, more diversified players with stronger sponsors and access to varied funding channels, including institutional private capital. FLCT's increasing aggregate leverage of 36.2% in 1QFY2025 places it at a disadvantage, reducing its ability to make accretive acquisitions or withstand further market shocks compared to peers with lower gearing and longer debt maturities. Interest Rate Sensitivity: Unprecedented Pressure on Cost of Capital

The sensitivity of REITs to interest rate movements has reached unprecedented levels, a critical factor underpinning our UNDERWEIGHT view on FLCT. For every 100 basis point increase in interest rates, a REIT's cost of capital typically rises, impacting borrowing costs, property valuations, and ultimately, DPU. With FLCT's S$2.4B in Total Debt as of FY2024, a significant portion is exposed to repricing due to its 2.0-year weighted average debt maturity.
