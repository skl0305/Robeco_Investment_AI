The unit economics are characterized by high fixed costs, long lead times for revenue ramp-up, and significant debt servicing obligations. The persistent negative free cash flow ($-1.3B in 2024) [Provided Financials] directly contradicts the market's implied view of a scalable, self-funding model. We project that without a fundamental shift in capital structure or a drastic increase in pricing power, this business model will continue to be a net consumer of capital, translating to further dilution for equity holders over the next 12-24 months, justifying our UNDERWEIGHT stance.

GDS's revenue mix, while diversified across major economic sectors like cloud services, internet, and finance, exhibits significant concentration risk within its hyperscale customer base. This will result in a lower-than-expected uplift to profitability from new AI workloads, eroding a key tenet of the bullish consensus and driving a $3-5/share negative re-rating for GDS as the market recognizes the limitations of its competitive position.
